Memory RAM

Memory RAM is one of the most components in determining your system's performance. RAM gives aplications a place to store and access data on short-term basis. The more programs your system is running, the more you will need.

If you want to reach peak performance you need to pair it with SSDs, the speed and performance of your PC directly correlates to the amount of RAM installed. Too little and you computer is slow and sluggish. You can install too much too for no added benefit.

The motherboard shows what type, how much capacity, how many slots and what speed it can support.

Nowadays with our technology and requirements a casual user would need at least 8GB RAM, an intermediate user 16GB and a professional user/gamer/graphic designer at least 32 GB. These are recommended, not necessary of course.

RAM speed means data transfer rate, the amount of data the RAM can pass to and from CPU. DDR means Double Data Rate and the number after is the generation(example DDR4). If you have more then the standard speed rating you to change how much your system is using from the BIOS.

Not the most expensive part of the system but a necessary one like many others. You need at least the minimum to be able to use a computer.
