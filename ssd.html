Solid-State Drive(SSD)

SSD are the superior version of the HDD in terms of speed, there is a big difference when having the operating system installed on SSD than on HDD.

There are different types of SSD, so be careful with the compatibility.

SATA SSD - SATA III slot, used on older laptops and desktops with performance of up to 550 MB/s.

M.2 SATA - SATA III slot, used on modern laptops and desktops, has performance of up to 550 MB/s.

M.2 NVMe - PCIe slot, for high performance laptops and desktops, with performance between 1500 MB/s to 7000 MB/s.

PCIe SSD - PCIe slot, for high performance desktops and workstations, the performance varies but it is several GB/s.

U.2 SSD - U.2 (PCIe) slot, used in enterprise/high-end consumer desktops, has very fast read/write speed of several GB/s.

NVMe SSD - PCIe (NVMe) slot, for high performance applications, with speed of several GB/s.

SSHD - SATA slot, for desktops and laptops, the speed is faster than HDDs but slower than SSDs.

The difference in the capacity of storage and speed increases like the price. You can use either just SSD, just HDD or even both, you can buy a cheap SSD with just enough space for the operating system.
